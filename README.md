## What Actually Drives Proxies Price (And Why Quotes Vary So Wildly)

A proxy is a middleman server that routes your web traffic through a different IP address. That's it. No magic. The proxies price youay reflects three things: where the IP comes from, how it's billed, and how clean its reputation is.

Three main categories show up on every provider's menu:

- **Datacenter proxies**: IPs hosted in commercial data centers. Cheap, fast, easy to spin up in bulk. Cost reflects this — pennies per IP.
- **Residential proxies**: IPs assigned by realISPs to real homes. Harder for websites to block. Cost reflects this too — usually billed by the gigabyte.
- **Static residential / ISP proxies**: A hybrid. Residential-grade IPs hosted on datacenter infrastructure. You get the trust profile of a residential IP and the speed of a datacenter, with pricing somewhere in the middle.

The reason quotes swing from $2 to $400 isn't shadiness, mostly. It's that these three categories solve genuinely different problems. A scraper hitting a site with no anti-bot defenses doesn't need the same proxy as someone trying to verify ad placements across50 countries from real residential IPs.

## How to Calculate Real Proxies Price (The Hidden Cost Trap)

The headline number on a pricing page is rarely the number you'll actually pay. Here's how to do the real math in five steps:

1. **Estimate monthly bandwidth honestly.** Open a browser tab, run your workflow for an hour, check the network tab. Multiply. Most people underestimate by 3x.
2. **Multiply by the per-GB rate at your spending tier**, not the cheapest advertised rate. The discount tiers usually require commitments that small operators won't hit.
3. **Add the per-IP cost** if your provider charges for both bandwidth and IP count. Many do quietly.
4. **Factor in failure rate.** A cheap proxy that's blocked 30% of the time is effectively 43% more expensive than its sticker price, because every retry burns more bandwidth.
5. **Compare aples to apples.** A $5/GB proxy with a 95% success rate beats a $3/GB proxy with a 70% success rate, every single time.

That fifth step is where most "cheap proxies" providers fall apart. The proxies price on the homepage is real, but the proxies price per successful request is what actually hits your wallet.
